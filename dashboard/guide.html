How it works

Each strategy is a rule for when to hold a stock vs. stay in cash. The system applies these rules to price data and shows you how they did historically (Backtests), what they signal today across a universe (Opportunities), and the signals drawn on a price chart (Charts). A company-strength score and news sentiment are shown alongside to help you judge each signal.

How often it changes

  • Live prices — about every 2 minutes during market hours.
  • News & Opportunities — hourly, 7am–9pm ET on weekdays.
  • Backtests, fundamentals, charts — once daily (4:30pm ET) and Saturday morning.

Signals recompute every refresh as prices move, so the Opportunities list shifts hourly. News is context and a filter today — not an automatic trade trigger.

The 5 strategies

1. Momentum — trend-following

Ride established uptrends. Rule: hold when the 20-day average is above the 50-day average, unless RSI > 75 (overbought). Wins in steady trends; whipsaws in choppy markets; lags at turns.

2. Breakout — trend-following (Donchian)

Buy strength at new highs. Rule: buy when price closes above the prior 20-day high; sell below the prior 10-day low. Catches big moves and cuts losers fast (low drawdowns); suffers false breakouts in flat markets. Often the best risk-adjusted performer here.

3. Mean-reversion — counter-trend (RSI)

Buy the dip, sell the recovery. Rule: buy when RSI < 30 (oversold); exit when RSI > 50. Shines in range-bound markets and bounces (protected capital in 2022); risky in real downtrends.

4. MACD — trend/momentum

Act when momentum turns up. Rule: hold when the MACD line (12- vs 26-day EMA) is above its 9-day signal line. Reacts faster than the SMA crossover; flip-flops in choppy markets.

5. Bollinger — counter-trend (volatility)

Buy when statistically cheap. Rule: bands = 20-day average ± 2 standard deviations; buy below the lower band, exit at the average. Good for volatile, mean-reverting names; struggles in sustained trends.

Families: trend-followers (momentum, breakout, MACD) ride moves and sit out downtrends; mean-reverters (RSI, Bollinger) buy weakness and sell into strength.

What the numbers mean

  • Total return — overall % gain/loss.
  • CAGR — return per year.
  • Max drawdown — worst peak-to-trough drop (the pain measure).
  • Sharpe — return per unit of risk; above 1 is good. Matters more than raw return.
  • Trades — round-trips (more = more fees).
  • Time in market — % of days holding vs. cash.
  • Buy & Hold — benchmark of simply holding.
  • Strength — 0–100 fundamentals quality. News mood — avg recent sentiment (−1 to +1).

How to use the dashboard

  • Opportunities — today's signals; filter to "Fresh buys" + "Strong companies" for a shortlist.
  • Charts — see where a strategy buys/sells on the price, with fundamentals.
  • Backtests — confirm historical behavior (watch the 2022 bear column).
  • News — market-moving headlines. Overview — live prices + strategy leaderboard.

Honest reminders

  • These are mechanical rules, not predictions — no edge is guaranteed.
  • High Sharpe + low drawdown beats chasing the highest return.
  • Backtests flatter reality (no fees/slippage). Paper-trade forward first.
  • Diversifying across strategies with strict risk limits beats any single "best" one.
