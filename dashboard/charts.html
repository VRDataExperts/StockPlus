What each line means

Close — the stock's actual daily price. This is what you'd pay.

SMA 20 — average price of the last 20 days (≈1 month). Shows the short-term trend.

SMA 50 — average of the last 50 days (≈2.5 months). The medium-term trend.

Bollinger bands (dashed) — SMA20 plus/minus 2 standard deviations. They widen when the stock is volatile and squeeze when calm.

▲Buy / ▼Sell — where the selected strategy would have entered/exited.

How to read it

Uptrend (bullish): price above both averages, and SMA20 above SMA50, both sloping up. Trend strategies want to be long here.

Downtrend (bearish): price below the averages and SMA20 below SMA50. Trend strategies stay in cash.

At the lower band: price is unusually cheap/oversold — a possible mean-reversion buy (expect a bounce).

At/above the upper band: price is stretched/expensive — strong trend, but risky to chase; a pullback is common.

Is it a good buy?

Higher confidence: uptrend (SMA20>SMA50) + a fresh green ▲ + strong fundamentals + price not far above the upper band.

Be cautious: price way above the upper band (overbought), or breaking below a falling SMA50 (trend weakening), or weak company score.

This describes how the indicators work — it is not advice. Signals fail often; size small and validate.
