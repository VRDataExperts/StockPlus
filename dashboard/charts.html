What each line means

Close — the stock's actual daily price. What you'd pay.

SMA 20 — average of the last 20 days (≈1 month); the short-term trend.

SMA 50 — average of the last 50 days (≈2.5 months); the medium-term trend.

Bollinger bands (dashed) — SMA20 ± 2 standard deviations; widen when volatile, squeeze when calm.

▲Buy / ▼Sell — where the selected strategy entered/exited (recent ~2 years).

How to read it

Uptrend (bullish): price above both averages, SMA20 above SMA50, both sloping up. Trend strategies want to be long.

Downtrend (bearish): price below the averages, SMA20 below SMA50. Trend strategies stay in cash.

At the lower band: unusually cheap/oversold — a possible mean-reversion buy (expect a bounce).

At/above the upper band: stretched/expensive — strong trend but risky to chase; a pullback is common.

Is it a good buy?

Higher confidence: uptrend (SMA20>SMA50) + a fresh green ▲ + strong fundamentals + price not far above the upper band.

Be cautious: price way above the upper band (overbought), breaking below a falling SMA50 (trend weakening), or a weak company score.

This describes how the indicators work — it is not advice. Signals fail often; size small and validate.
