What the horizons mean

Day — fastest settings (≈3/8-day averages); reacts quickest, most false signals. Hold days.

Week — medium settings (≈5/15-day); balances speed and noise. Hold ~1–3 weeks.

Month — slower settings (≈20/50-day); fewer, steadier signals. Hold weeks–months.

How confidence is scored

Starts at 50, then adjusts for: trend (price vs its averages), momentum (recent return), volume surge (unusual buying), RSI (penalty if overbought), and news sentiment. A fresh buy adds a little.

≥70 = strong alignment. 50–69 = mixed. <50 = weak — caution.

Why / when to be careful

Volatile, low-priced stocks can move 10%+ in a day — great for gains, brutal on losses. High volatility = wide stops and small position sizes.

Confidence is a setup score, not a prediction. Many signals fail. Always decide your exit before entering, and never risk more than you can lose.
